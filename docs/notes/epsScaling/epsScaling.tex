\title{DPG for convection-diffusion: distribution of $\epsilon$}

\begin{document}
The first order system for convection-diffusion can be written in a variety of ways.  For $a,b\in [0,1]$, we can distribute the $\epsilon$ factor 
\begin{align*}
\div \LRp{\beta u - \epsilon^a\sigma} &= f \\
\frac{1}{\epsilon^b} - \epsilon^{(1-a-b)} \grad u &= 0.
\end{align*}
$a$ controls the distribution of $\epsilon$ into the conservation equation, while $b$ determines the distribution of $\epsilon$ onto either the $\sigma$ or $\grad u$ term in the stress equation.  

Demkowicz and Heuer use $a = 0$, $b = 1$, while Broersen and Stevenson use the choice of $a = 1/2$ and $b = 0$, noting that, as $\epsilon \rightarrow 0$, the first order system decouples into the pure convection equation for $u$ and a trivial equation for $\sigma$.  

So long as $a+b<1$, then the trace of $u$ resulting from integration by parts of $\epsilon^{(1-a-b)} \grad u$ will carry with it a power of $\epsilon$, such that, as $\epsilon \rightarrow 0$, the outflow boundary condition will release in the limit.
